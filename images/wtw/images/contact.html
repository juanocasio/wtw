You may know Rev. Dr. Wyatt Tee Walker only through what you've read in books or seen online. You may have been able to work alongside - or witness - him as he served as a civil rights leader, theologian,
or Chief of Staff to Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.

Maybe Rev. Walker was your pastor (as he was mine at Canaan Baptist Church - pastor, "father," friend, supportive counsel and more).
Or maybe you heard his dynamic sermons followed by
his "singing" of old time gospel favorites.

You may have journeyed with him to foreign shores,
or relaxed on gentle waves boating at the NJ marina.

Perhaps you were his student at various theological institutions or
via his writings and community activism, speeches and service. Or
maybe Rev. Walker inspired you to be a better pastor/preacher.

Note: The site's administrator is Dr. Melody T. McCloud, an obstetrician-gynecologist and author in Atlanta, GA. Dr. McCloud was a member of Canaan Baptist Church in the early 1970s. Rev. Walker appointed Melody secretary to the Music Department (under Clinton Utterbach and Gene Cooper) as he was working on his doctoral studies on sacred music. Rev. Walker also asked Melody to help him create and layout the Deacon Parishes, that the church still uses to this day. Melody was a member of the Celestial Choir, the Canaan Concert Choir and other church ministries. She is the author of BLESSED HEALTH: The African-American Woman's Guide to Physical & Spiritual Well-being (Simon & Schuster). Web site: www.DrMcCloud.com.
